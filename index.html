The Problem

Bharat Doesn't Lack Riders.
It Lacks 24×7 Mechanic Uncles.

The rider on a Ballia service road at 9:47 PM with white smoke pouring from their exhaust, who doesn't speak fluent English, can't read a 200-page PDF.

"yeh kya ho raha hai bhai?"

They photograph the smoke, hit the mic button — and get an answer in Hindi in 4 seconds, grounded in the actual manual, with the option to listen to the reply being read out.

— The Mechanic Saathi vision
📖

200-Page PDF Manuals

No one reads the full owner's manual — especially not when stranded on the road at night with a malfunctioning bike.

🌐

Language Barrier

Most manuals are in English. Most riders speak Hindi, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, or Bengali. The gap is massive.

🕐

No Mechanic at Night

Nearest mechanic is closed. YouTube videos buffer on 2G. Forums give conflicting advice. The rider is alone.

🤖

AI Hallucinations Kill Trust

Generic chatbots guess answers. A wrong repair instruction can damage an engine or risk safety. Trust requires citations.

Core Features

Three Pillars of
Mechanic Saathi

Every answer is grounded, every interaction is multilingual, every input mode just works.

📖

Strictly Grounded

Every answer cites a chunk ID from the manual. If retrieval scores below threshold, the bot refuses — "yeh manual mein nahi mila" — instead of hallucinating. Zero tolerance for made-up answers.

🎤

Fully Sarvam Voice Stack

Sarvam STT (Saarika) detects language, Sarvam-m generates answers natively, Bulbul speaks the reply back, Mayura translates on demand. Complete Indic voice AI.

🌍

Multimodal Input Bar

Mic, image, and text input live in a single bar. The image flows through a vision model into an English symptom description that joins the question before retrieval.
